Roll the barbell up over your thighs, until it is sitting on your hips. Plant your feet on the floor, make sure your feet are about shoulder width apart while bending your knees. Place your hands on the bar with an overhand grip to stabilize the bar resting on your hips. Begin to push the bar towards the ceiling by extending your hips. Note that your knees should form about a 90 degree angle at the height of your lift. Lower the weight so your glutes are just barely floating above the ground and repeat for reps.|
Lateral Walk With Band|Find an elastic band and place an elastic band around your ankles. Now begin to walk sideways, note to do this you have to bring your leg out against the band’s resistance. Now you can do this exercise in two ways. You can change your direction after every step, or you can continue to take steps in one direction for a certain amount of steps. Repeat for reps.|
Machine Glute Kickbacks|Find the Glute Kickback machine in your gym and adjust the machine to your body. Note that your knees should be facing forward and resting near the bottom of the padding. Then place your forearms on the upper padding and grip the handles. Next you want to make sure that you have the arm of the machine under your knee. Now keep your body still, and start to press your leg backwards and upwards by activating the glute. Try to push your legs as far up as you can without rotating your hips, then return to the starting position. Do all reps on one leg first, and then switch to the other leg and repeat.|
One-Legged Glute Bridge|Find a place where you can comfortably lie down on the ground, you will want one foot on the floor, and the other leg extended. You will need to tuck your pelvis inwards properly so you can activate the glutes. Begin to push your hips towards the ceiling by using the glute muscle in the bent leg. Push yourself upwards until your body forms a straight line from head to foot. At the height of your movement you will want to squeeze your glutes. Once you have finished your reps with that leg, switch legs and repeat for reps.|
One-Legged Hip Thrust|Find a chair, couch or a bench. You will begin to lean your back against your chair, sofa, or bench. Use whatever you have on hand and whatever you find to be the most comfortable. Plant one foot on the floor and lift the other leg up. Now begin to push your hips towards the ceiling, you want to use your glute muscle in the leg that is touching the floor. The leg that is pushing you up should form around a 90-degree angle at the height of your movement. Lower your hips back down to the starting position and repeat for reps.|
Romanian Deadlift|Grab a bar or barbell and get into the starting position by deadlifting a barbell off the floor, or by unracking it from a barbell rack. Begin to lean forward by hinging in your hips. While doing this try to keep your knees almost completely extended. Now lean forward as far as possible without rounding your back. Note that you don't have to touch the barbell to the floor, however if you do that is absolutely fine. Once you are standing straight begin to reverse the movement with control and return to your starting position. Repeat for reps.|
Single Leg Romanian Deadlift|Grab a bar or barbell and stand upright and hold the bar with your hands so they are about shoulder-width apart. Now lift one of your legs off the ground. It can rest on the ground as you stand there. Once you start the lift keep the back straight and start to lean forward by hinging at the hips. As you lean forward one of your legs will move backwards and upwards. Now lower yourself until you feel a stretch in the standing leg hamstring. Try to make sure to keep your hips still, the last thing you want is for the side with your lifted leg to start rotating upwards. Return to the starting position. Finish all your reps on one side first, and then repeat on the other leg.|
Standing Glute Kickback in Machine|Find the Standing Glute Kickback machine in your gym and adjust the machine to your body. Note that your knees should be facing forward and resting near the bottom of the padding. Place your forearms on the upper padding and grip the handles. Next you want to make sure that you have the arm of the machine under your knee. Keep your body still, and start to press your leg backwards and upwards by activating the glute. Try to push the leg as far up as you can without rotating your hips, then once at the height of your movement return your leg on the pad to the starting position. Do all your reps on one of your legs first, and then switch to the other leg.|
Step up|Stand in front of a bench, box or even a chair. Please make sure whatever you are stepping up to can hold your weight and is stable. Place one of your feet onto the object you will be stepping up to. Now step up to the object until your leg is straight. Lower yourself wit control until your other foot has touched the ground. Keep in mind you want to keep your other foot on the object at all times. Repeat for reps.|
Cable Crunch|Fasten a rope handle in the upper position on a cable pulley. Sit down on your knees and make sure you are a few feet away from the pulley and make sure you are facing the pulley. Bend your upper body forward and hold the ropes on either side of your head and then pull downwards until your head is barely floating above the ground. Now reverse the motion and return to the starting position with control.|
Crunch|Find a comfortable place to lie on your back, place your hands in front of your chest and make sure your knees are bent to about 90 degrees. Lift your upper body by contracting your abs and bending forward. Try to bend as far forward as possible while still keeping your lower back on the floor and then return to the starting position. Repeat for reps|
Dead Bug|Find a comfortable place to lie on your back, and keep your arms straight up towards the ceiling and your legs stacked over your hips, try to keep your knees bent at a 90-degree angle. Make sure that the lower back has contact with the surface. Now with control straighten out your right leg and lower it towards the floor, while lowering your leg you will lower your opposite arm over your head as far as you can. While this is happening keep the lower back in contact with the floor the entire time. Reverse the movement, and repeat for reps and make sure to switch legs and arms to perform reps for the other side.|
Hanging Leg Raise|Jump up and grab a bar, make sure you are hanging high enough from the ground so that you can hang from it with straight legs. Now begin to lift your legs as high as you can in front of you, make sure you're not swinging when performing this action. Begin to lower your legs again, with control. Repeat for reps.|
Hanging Knee Raise|Jump up and grab a bar, make sure you are hanging high enough from the ground so that you can hang from it with straight legs. Now begin to lift your knees as high as you can in front of you. Try to line your knee up with your chest and while performing the motion make sure you aren't swinging. Lower your legs again, with control and repeat for reps.|
Hanging Sit-Up|Try to find a hanging sit-up machine in your gym. Now begin to climb into position and lower your upper body down to the starting position, try to keep your hands crossed over your chest. Lift your upper body upwards and then at the top of your movement begin to reverse the motion and lower yourself down to the starting position again. Repeat for reps.|
High to Low Wood Chop with Band|Grab an elastic band and fasten it high up. Grip the band with both hands, take about a step away from the band, and stand sideways to the band’s anchor point. With almost straight arms, make a sweeping, chopping-like movement diagonally downward. Return to the starting position in a controlled manner. Repeat for reps.|
Horizontal Wood Chop with Band|Grab an elastic band and fasten the band at shoulder height. Grip the band with both hands, take about a step away from the band, and stand sideways to the band’s anchor point. With almost straight arms, make a sweeping, horizontal movement to your other side. Return to the starting position in a controlled manner. Repeat for reps.|
Kneeling Ab Wheel Roll-Out|Grab a soft pad to place your knees, and place the ab wheel on the floor in front of you. Try to roll out as far as you can, and while doing so try to maintain a straight back throughout the entire movement. Reverse the movement with control, and return to the starting position. Repeat for reps. |
Kneeling Plank|Find a comfortable place on the ground where you can lie on your elbows and knees. Push your body upwards like a push-up but instead of going downwards again you will sit at the top of that exercise. Try to form and hold a straight line from your head to hips. Once you have planked for a specific amount of time, rest up and repeat for reps|
Kneeling Side Plank|Find a comfortable place on the ground where you can stand on your side, and you will lean against one elbow and your knee. Your core should be facing the same direction your head is facing. Now try to form and hold a straight line from your head to knees. Once you have planked for a specific amount of time, rest up and repeat for reps.|
Lying Leg Raise|Find a comfortable place to lie down on the ground, you will have your back on the floor, and your arms at your sides. With straight legs, lift your legs until they are pointing straight up, keep in mind that you want to keep your lower back attached to floor. Lower your legs again, with control. Repeat for reps.|
Lying Windshield Wiper|Find a comfortable place to lie down on your back, make sure your arms are out to your sides and your legs straight up in the air. Begin to slowly lower your legs down to one side, while this happening try to keep your legs as straight as possible. Then bring your legs back to your starting position so they are straight up in the air again and lower your legs to the other side. Reverse this motion again and repeat for reps.|
Lying Windshield Wiper with Bent Knees|Find a comfortable place to lie down on your back, make sure your arms are out to your sides and your legs straight up in the air. After you have assumed that position bend your knees to 90 degrees. Begin to slowly lower your legs down to one side. Then bring your legs back to your starting position and lower your legs to the opposite side. Reverse this motion again and repeat for reps.|
Machine Crunch|Find the crunch machine in your gym and adjust the machine to the appropriate settings for you, sit down and grip the handles. Bend forward as far as you can then reverse the motion and return to the starting position with control. Repeat for reps|
Mountain Climbers|Get into the plank position and make sure your hands are about shoulder-width apart, try to keep your back as flat as possible. Begin by pulling your right knee into your chest as far as you can. Then switch legs, this means you will pull your opposite knee inwards towards your chest while your other knee outwards to its starting position. While performing your mountain climbers keep your hips down and run the knees in and out as far and as fast as you can. Repeat for reps.|
Oblique Crunch|Find a comfortable place to lie on your back, now position your hands on the side of your head, and make sure that with your feet planted bend your knees to about a 90 degree angle. Now begin to lift your upper body diagonally, do this in a way so that your elbow and shoulder on one side of your body move towards the knee that is opposite of your elbow/shoulder. Bend as far as possible and then return to the starting position. Repeat for reps and remember to switch which elbow comes up everytime.|
Oblique Sit-Up|Grab two dumbbells and find a comfortable place to lie on your back, place your hands behind your head, and bend your knees to about 90 degrees. Use the dumbbells you grabbed and stick your feet underneath them, you want to do this so your feet don't lift from the ground. Now begin to lift your upper body diagonally by bending forward, bend forwards until your right elbow touches your left leg, or vice versa. Then return to the starting position. Remember that you will want to switch which elbow comes upwards in this exercise, repeat for reps.|
Plank|Find a comfortable place to plank, now stand on your elbows and feet. Now try to form and hold a straight line from your head to feet and hold that for as long as you desired. Repeat for reps.|
Side Plank|Find a comfortable place on the ground to stand on your side, leaning against one elbow. Place the foot that is not touching the ground in front of the top leg in front of the other foot. Now try to form and hold a straight line from your head to your feet. Hold that for as long as you can, repeat for reps.|
Sit-Up|Grab two dumbbells or a any weight to keep down your legs while performing the exercise. Find a comfortable place to lie on your back, with your hands in front of your chest and your knees bent to about 90 degrees. Use the weight you grabbed and stick your feet under it so they don't lift from the ground. Begin to lift your upper body by bending forward. Try to bend as far forward as possible, and then return to the starting position. Repeat for reps.|
Eccentric Heel Drop|Grab a stair step or even a thick weight plate, make sure you can stand on this elevated surface stably. If need be find something that you can hold on to for balance. Begin to raise your heels and extend your feet by using both your legs. Lift one leg in the air, and then lower yourself with control standing on only one foot. Make sure to switch to the opposite foot and repeat for reps.|
Heel Raise|Grab a solid surface that you can stand on and make sure it slightly elevated. If need be find something to hold on to for balance. Now begin to raise your heels by using your calves. Now lower yourself with control, and repeat for reps.|
Seated Calf Raise|Find the seated calf raise machine in your gym and sit down on the seat, and adjust the knee pads to your correct height. Now place your toes and the ball of your feet on the foot support. Begin to lower the weight by bending your ankles in a controlled movement. Then push the weight up by extending your ankles. Repeat for reps.|
Standing Calf Raise|Find the standing calf raise machine and place your toes and the ball of your feet on the foot support. Place the shoulder pads against your shoulders and stand upright in the starting position. Now begin to lower yourself down by bending your ankles in a controlled movement. Now to push yourself up by extending your ankles. Repeat for reps.|
Barbell Wrist Curl|Grab a barbell with a underhand grip that is about shoulder width apart, make sure to rest your forearms against your thighs, or alternatively against a bench. Lower your hands towards the floor, and let the barbell roll out into your fingers. Now begin to reverse this motion begin by closing your grip and bending your wrists upwards. Repeat for reps.|
Barbell Wrist Curl Behind the Back|Grab a barbell and hold it behind your back and let your arms hang with the barbell in hand. Now begin to open your hands and let the barbell roll out in your fingers. Now begin to reverse this motion begin by closing your grip and bending your wrists upwards. Repeat for reps.|
Bar Hang|Find a bar in your gym that you can grab onto and hang onto it so your feet don't touch the ground. Now just hang there and you can experiment with relaxed or active shoulders.|
Dumbbell Wrist Curl|Grab a dumbbell with an underhand grip and rest your forearm against your thigh, or alternatively against a bench. Now begin to lower your hand towards the floor, and let the dumbbell roll out into your fingers. Now to reverse this motion begin by closing your grip and bending your wrist upwards.|
Farmers Walk|Step in between two farmers walk-cases, or grab two kettlebells at the same weight. Now grip the handles and and lift the weights. Keep your head looking straight in front of you and begin to move forward in small steps. Increase the stride length as you increase the speed. Try to keep your body in a straight line as you are walking try not to lean excessively forward as you walk. When you are done, lower the weights back to the ground in a controlled manner. Repeat for reps.|
Fat Bar Deadlift|Grab a barbell or bar and step up close to the bar, make sure the bar is sitting just over the middle of your foot. Begin to lean forward and grip the bar, then lift the bar. Try to pull the bar close to your body, all while maintaining a straight back, until you are standing straight. Lower the bar back to the ground with control. Repeat for reps.|
Gripper|Grab a gripper in your gym and hold it so that it is as open as possible, make sure it is not slipping out of your hand. Close the gripper all the way by closing your hand. Return to the starting position and repeat for reps.|
One-Handed Bar Hang|Find a bar in your gym that you can grab onto and hang onto it so that your feet don't touch the ground. Grab a bar with one hand, and hang from it. Start off by hanging with a tensed and active shoulder, and then gradually experiment with hanging with a more relaxed shoulder. Remember to switch which hand you hang from once you finish a set.|
Plate Pinch|Grab a pair of weight plates in a pinching grip. Lift the weight plates off the ground. Hold them for as long as you can, and then put them down in a controlled manner. Remember to switch which hand you grip the plates with as you finish a set.|
Plate Wrist Curl|Grab a small weight plate with a pinching underhand grip and rest your forearm against your thigh, or alternatively against a bench. Begin to lower your hand towards the floor. Now begin to reverse this motion by bending your wrist upwards. Repeat for reps.|
Towel Pull-Up|Lay a pair of towels over a pull-up bar. Grab one towel in each hand. Begin to pull yourself up as high as you can. At the height of your motion begin to lower yourself with control until your arms are fully extended. Repeat for reps.|
